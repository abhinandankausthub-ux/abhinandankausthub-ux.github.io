About

I am a Master's student in Aerospace Engineering at Auburn University specializing in structural integrity, fatigue & fracture mechanics, and composite materials. My work focuses on predicting material failure, crack propagation, and reliability of engineering systems under realistic loading conditions.

I have developed advanced engineering tools that simulate fatigue crack growth using Paris, Walker, and Forman models, as well as composite laminate behavior using Classical Lamination Theory (CLT). These tools integrate analytical modeling, numerical simulation, and data-driven approaches to solve real-world structural problems.

My technical experience spans finite element analysis, fatigue life prediction, fracture mechanics, and composite failure modeling, with hands-on experience in Python, ANSYS, and engineering simulation workflows.

I am actively seeking opportunities where I can apply my expertise in structural mechanics to solve high-impact engineering challenges in aerospace, energy, and advanced manufacturing industries.
